The Governing Council of the Federal University, Oye-Ekiti (FUOYE), has appointed Professor Joshua Olalekan Ogunwole as the institution's 5th substantive Vice-Chancellor. His appointment was announced on Thursday following a Council meeting held at the National Open University of Nigeria (NOUN) main campus in Abuja.

The announcement was made by the Chairman of the Governing Council, Senator Victor Ndoma-Egba (SAN), who highlighted the integrity of the selection process.

Senator Ndoma-Egba disclosed that the selection, which saw 64 candidates apply for the position (including two expatriates), was thorough, rigorous, and fully digitalized marking it as the first of its kind in the Nigerian university system.

He explained that the digital assessments were meticulously complemented with manual verification to ensure complete accuracy and transparency throughout the process. Following a systematic review and security screening by the Department of State Services (DSS) in Ekiti State, Professor Ogunwole was approved as the most qualified candidate for the role.

Professor Ogunwole, a renowned scholar and Professor of Soil Science, currently serves as FUOYE’s Deputy Vice-Chancellor in charge of Strategic Partnerships, Research, Innovation and Linkages.

* Assumption of Office: He will officially assume his duties on February 11, 2026.
* Succession: He succeeds the outgoing Vice-Chancellor, Professor Abayomi Sunday Fasina, whose tenure concludes on February 10, 2026.
* Academic Background: Professor Ogunwole holds Bachelor’s, Master’s, and Doctoral degrees from Ahmadu Bello University, Zaria.
* Experience: His nearly 30 years of professional experience include roles at various institutions, such as Adamawa State University, Federal University Dutsin-Ma, and Bowen University.

The Council also approved several other key appointments, including the University Librarian, Director of Health Services, Director of Works and Services, and Director of Physical Planning, in an effort to strengthen FUOYE’s institutional leadership.
